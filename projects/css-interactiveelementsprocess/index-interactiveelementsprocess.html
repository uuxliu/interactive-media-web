INTERACTIVE MEDIA: WEB

INTERACTIVE ELEMENTS PROCESS

THE BEGINNING

FIRST IMPRESSIONS: When I first found out that my assigned interactions for the Interative Elements project was show/hide, I was very lost. Besides the time/duration interaction, I thought the other interactions like click or hover were much more straightforward to manipulate and play around with. These were also interactions that I'm most used to and familiar with. My interaction was a little conceptual in it of its own. I thought it was very difficult to illustrate the concept of showing and hiding content in a creative way so finding a way around this was maybe the hardest obstacle I saw coming ahead for me.

BRAINSTORMING IDEAS: I moved onto brainstorming ideas of how content or information is being transmitted to me on a daily basis and how I interact with it online. I also google searched some key terms that talked about the feautures of showing and hiding content online to audiences in order to get a quick visual grasp on my interactive element. I found that most of the things I've noted down for my show/hide element is very dependent on the other interactive elements that were assignmented to the other students in the course.

REFLECTIION: I wanted to reflect on what I've gathered from brainstorming so that I can organize my ideas and thoughts. I was challenged by the fact that my element was very dependent on other interactive elements and not having a clear interaction of its own. The challenge I thought to myself was how can I create a website that's solely focused on one thing when it's conceptual abstract in it of itself. And when the interactive element is solely about the visual aspects of something, it makes it harder to be creativity when something is also hidden.

INSPIRATIONS: I looked around the web to be inspired from what show/hide interactions that others have implemented into their websites. It also helped that I kept in mind which audiences they were speeaking to because I believe the interfaces and specific target audience greatly affected which effects they've chosen to use. Some resources or inspirations were direct tutorials allowing and helping me to be able to develop my abstract website, like the implementation of buttons. Others were websites that taught me how to visual rely information to the viewer in a creative and effect way. These sites also taught me how how to conceptually use my interactive element to convey a message indirectly to the audience.




THE MIDDLE

IDEAS: After having done some external investigations on how others have incorporated the show/hide interaction, I started to think about how I can incorporate the interaction to better the concept that will be connected to my abstract website. I had 2 initial ideas I wanted to go for but I had to weigh how the concepts related and communicated my hide/show interaction to the user in a clear and consise way. Even though it is an 'abstract' website, I want my interaction to be incorporated clearly.

IDEA 1, POP-UPS: My 1st idea was utilizing pop-up messages and windows where the user had to open and close them in a short period of time and this would greatly communicate my interaction. I wanted to illustrate the invasive and annoying effects of marketing and sales that call for consumer's and their attention when roaming in the malls. By having colourful displays, flashing signs, and a fast environment, my abstract website would also be an abstract imitation or representation of online consumerism as well.

IDEA 2, CARNIVAL: Through personal and researching experiences on the web, I find that the hide/show interaction is greatly affected or triggered by movements and interactions made by the curser, and thereby the mouse/trackpad. My 2nd idea is a carnival where an array of intereactions are used to communicate my hide/show interaction. Each interaction would trigger something to be shown or hidden and would connect to a carnival theme, in terms of colour, messages or even mini games.

MY DECISION: I chosee to go with the carnival idea because rather than showcasing only 1 way of showing or hiding content, I think showing the array of different display methods that affects how information can be read could communicate my element in a more better, effective way. By having multiple different interactions that show/hide content, it puts more emphasis on the effects of the elements rather than what initiates it all becaus the end outcome are similarly connected.




THE END?

AN ARRAY OF INTERACTIONS: I then searched for different methods to show or hide content that's inspired from the web, the list that I brainstormed and a combination of both. I also wanted to keep my interactions consistant and relate it to the theme of a carnival as well so the activities in the end would mimic messages or games evident in a curious and somewhat strange carnival.

GATHERING RESOURCES: After planning out my interactions, I began to search for resources from websites such as W3Schools, Code Pen, and Youtube in order to implement my ideas, as well as troubleshoot my ideas along the way. Most notably I tried to be inspired from existing examples to further my knowledge within HTML, CSS and with newly learned knowledge from JS. Although JS is to be implemented, I still found myself straying away from formally using it in an extravagant and repeatedly way beecausee the language it uses is quite confusing for me to grasp at the moment.
