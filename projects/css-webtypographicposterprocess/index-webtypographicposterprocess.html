INTERACTIVE MEDIA: WEB

WEB TYPOGRAPHIC POSTER PROCESS

THE BEGINNING

RESEARCH: I started the Web Typographic Poster project by picking the Rhode Island School of Design package. Upon hearing the school's name, I visualized a modern art and design school placed on an island. This imagery peaked my curiosity and so I decided to go with it. I did some preliminary reseearch on their website, which can be found here.

INSPIRATION 1: I drew upon different themes and places for inspiration. From the school's webpage, there is a public pdf of their vision spanning from 2020 to 2027. I thought this document very interesting because it's a proposal created by members of the RISD, which will help me see how they design and what their brand identity is. The thing that struck me most was the simple, modern layout that's accompanied by a stunning gradient. The pdf can be found here. I also found their home page, which showcases different rotating art work upon refreshing the page, very stunning as well.

INSPIRATION 2: Upon hearing the word 'island', I immediately thought and visualized a poster that facilitates a fun adventure surrounding some sort of map. The map would have suggestive images and colours that create a 'lost at sea' or a 'finding teasure' concept. The inspirational images below were are all found on Google Images.

OTHER INSPIRATIONS: All other inspirations and references I had for my Web Typographic Poster were Google Images I've saved of modern typographic posters. I took a look at how I should put and incorporate images and informational content with evident typographic hierarchy.




ROUGH IDEAS

NOTES: I jotted down some of the rough ideas I had from the inspirations/references I mentioned above. It was a way for me to organize some of the bigger concepts that I had and figure out how I can use the ideas in the most effective way.

SKETCHES: These are my explorations from trying to figure out a way to use or even to merge my big ideas together. I tried to brainstorm other ways to further expand the thoughts I had. Some of the words and terms that struck out to me from researching were: discover and transmit, global society, wider world, collabprative interplay, diverse methods, intergrate, and community.




THE MIDDLE

ON MY WAY: After refining the concept that I wanted my web typographic poster to have, I drew out a genral idea sketch and laid out the hierarchies and body copies of the content package.




THE END?

EXPLORATION: After laying out my body copy, I started to look around for more innovative and interesting effects that i can incorporate into my Web Typographic Poster, such as animations, buttons, font and more. I wanted the poster to have my original ideas but making it more refined because unlike an abstract fine arts school, RISD from my reseach is more refined and higher class than other arts facilities I've heard about.
